Are We There Yet?

Creative Thinking, Strategy, Web Design | January - May 2015
The Challenge

Can positive reinforcement of existing environmentally friendly behavior motivate college students to form sustainable transportation habits?

DEVELOPING A PSYCHOLOGY EXPERIMENT

After reaching out to professionals and preforming on the ground research, we decided to begin ideating possibilities for what our “evaluation” would be. After lots of conversation, sticky noting and white boarding, we began forming a plan around a simulated scenario in which a participant would make transportation decisions, along with an environmental assessment. With this new plan, we assume that the user is echoing potential decisions they would make in real life.

The Team

“Are We There Yet?” is a team project I worked on with students in Scout, Northeastern’s student-lead design firm. I was involved in all aspects of planning, but dedicated most of my time to the design of the scenario above.

The Solution

Our experiment consists of an experimental group and a control group- both college age students. To begin the experiment, both groups would run through a web-based scenario that we designed. The scenario simulates what it's like to move to a new city after graduating college. Participants will make transportation decisions that will help us to calculate how “green” they might be in real life. Then for the next three months, the experimental group will receive positive reinforcement about their real life behavior. The control group will receive no positive reinforcement. After 3 months, both groups will take the same simulation and we will calculate if the experimental group makes greener choices.
